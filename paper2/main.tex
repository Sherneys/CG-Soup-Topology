\begin{abstract}
Resampling priors can steer \emph{where} a differentiable triangle-soup
reconstruction spends its triangle budget, but our controlled study of that
channel (supplementary material) found their topological value carried
largely by spatial \emph{width}, and no prior shape repairs
one-dimensional features. We move topology
from the allocation channel into the \emph{objective}:
$L=\mathrm{photometric}+\lambda(t)\,L_{\mathrm{topo}}$, where
$L_{\mathrm{topo}}$ is an optimally-matched distance between the live surface's
persistence diagram and a fixed target diagram. Gradients flow through a
pair-frozen backward: matched birth/death simplices re-expressed as
closed-form circumradii of live sample positions (exact for Gabriel
simplices --- all significant pairs in practice), plus a recruitment term
restoring the gradient optimal matching provably lacks when a target
feature is missing; one ratio knob $\rho$ sets the scale by
gradient-norm calibration, and a curriculum proved unnecessary.
% src: PHASE3_PLAN.md Appendix D / topo3/report/results.json
On budget-starved synthetic scenes the loss is topology-specific where a
norm-matched non-topological control is not: enclosed voids improve
$4.0$--$7.9\times$ and --- the channel's distinguishing result --- torus loops
improve $2.3\times$ with zero phantom handles across seeds, while the control
collapses a loop. The channels compose: loss plus the best prior reaches
$7.6\times$ below baseline at the best Chamfer. Component
counting (H0) is again matched by the generic control --- this class needs
no topological information. All verdicts
replicate, with no per-shape tuning, on four external genus-known meshes
--- one a raw artist mesh ingested and \emph{certified} rather than
constructed --- at $2.1$--$10.4\times$ and equal-or-better Chamfer, and the
loss degrades gracefully (never below baseline) under sensor noise up to
one sample spacing.
% src: topo3/report/results.json (spot/bob/fandisk) +
% topo3/quicklook.json (kinkin, 2026-07-10)
Prescription: correct topology in the loss, allocate with a wide prior, and
combine.
\end{abstract}
