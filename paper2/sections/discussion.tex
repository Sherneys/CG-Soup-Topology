\paragraph{Sparse tugs vs.\ budget allocation.} Each significant pair
back-propagates through the $3$--$7$ sample positions of its birth and
death simplices: \emph{precise but sparse} pulls, iterated by re-pairing
--- the toys show surgery ``walking'' around a defect a few points at a
time; the resampling prior is the opposite --- broad, indirect,
budget-limited. The results sort cleanly along this axis: 1-D loops, which
allocation can only over- or under-tessellate, respond to value pressure
(H1 win); wide 2-D shells benefit from both (prior helps, loss helps more,
composition best); 0-D structure needs neither (H0 null, three channels).

\paragraph{Why the control damages topology.} The norm-matched repulsion
imposes a length-scale floor on the sampled surface: the void's death
pins at that equilibrium (identical across seeds), then drags, while
near-diagonal phantom bars accumulate (diagram trajectories: suppl.\
Fig.~S1), and thin structures collapse ($\beta_2$ on the cube, $\beta_1$
on the torus). The loss has no length-scale constraint --- it pulls the
death coordinate itself and the geometry follows. This sharpens the
control argument: spending the same gradient budget through the same
channel \emph{without} topological information is not neutral but
harmful, so C1's wins cannot be an artifact of extra regularization.

\paragraph{Measurability at the loss's density.} A persistence loss can only
enforce features that clear the significance floor at its sampling density
$M$: the torus void and the double torus's two tube loops do not, at
$M{=}2048$. We treat this as a principle, not a nuisance: the target
bundle is built (and audited) at the same $M$, features below the floor are
excluded from the objective, and the double torus is evaluated
against its two measurable loops --- also all the reconstruction
itself can express at feasible budgets.

The floor is quantifiable: a bar is significant iff its lifetime exceeds
$6\,r_{\mathrm{med}}(M)$ ($r_{\mathrm{med}}$ = median nearest-neighbour
spacing, the implementation's self-calibrating threshold), and
$r_{\mathrm{med}}$ follows the uniform-sampling law $\propto M^{-1/2}$
(measured $.0090{\to}.0028$ over $M{=}2048{\to}20000$; ratio $3.16$ vs
$\sqrt{9.77}{=}3.13$). The
double torus's tube loops live at lifetime $.045$--$.046$ on the clean
surface: at $M{=}2048$ that is $0.83$--$0.85\times$ the floor --- invisible
by a $15\%$ margin, clearing it only from $M\!\approx\!3{\times}10^{3}$ ---
while at the evaluation density ($M{=}20000$, floor $.0171$) they clear it
$3.1\times$; the binding constraint there is the \emph{representation}
(the soup never expresses the tubes), so raising $M$
alone cannot close the gap. The torus quantifies the interaction with
noise: its second loop clears the clean floor by only $1.33\times$, a
margin the live cloud's noise consumes --- why it is chronically
sub-threshold in training and recruitment is its only gradient path (C6).
Recruitment is thus how the loss reaches below its own significance floor
on the live side --- landscape-guided in practice (unproductive tears
self-extinguish at the local detour scale; suppl.\ \S C), with the
location-blindness caveat standing for target features larger than any
achievable detour.
Suppl.\ Table~S3 tabulates the counts across $M$ (at $4096$ all four
double-torus loops clear). The tom-yum pot ran the rule
\emph{prospectively} (\S\ref{sec:setup}): it picked observable class and
bundle density before any training run; the in-loop result
(Table~\ref{tab:gen}) validated it. Adaptive or multi-scale density is
the natural extension; the trade is explicit, since persistence cost
grows $O(M\log M)$.
