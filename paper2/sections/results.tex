term, Adam on point positions) repairs defective clouds on all
four controlled probes --- a punctured sphere's void closes ($8.8\times$),

\paragraph{Voids (H2): topology-specific, large.} Sphere $4.0\times$
($16.2\sigma$ Welch vs C0), cube $7.9\times$ ($18.5\sigma$), both at
equal-or-better Chamfer (sphere $0.46$ vs $0.50$; cube $0.92$ vs $1.05$). The
norm-matched control is not merely weaker --- it is \emph{destructive}: it
pins the void's death at its repulsion equilibrium ($2.2\times$ worse than
baseline, identical across seeds) and on the cube erases the void
outright. Nor is this control-strength dependent: a gentle variant at
half the push radius still lands $1.6\times$ \emph{worse}
(.0972 vs .0623) --- the ordering is C1 $<$ C0 $<$ C2g $<$ C2, so no
tested strength reproduces any of the gain
(per-seed spread: suppl.\ Fig.~S3; pin-and-drag mechanism:
\S\ref{sec:discussion}).
% src: topo3/report/results.json (sphere: C2g 0.0972+-0.0169)

\paragraph{Loops (H1): the channel's distinguishing result.} In the
allocation study (suppl.\ \S A) no prior shape won this class:
concentration manufactured phantom handles; spreading never beat a random
control. The loss cuts torus
H1 error $2.3\times$ with \emph{zero phantom handles in all five seeds}
(significant-count trajectories flat at the true $\beta_1{=}2$,
suppl.\ Fig.~S5), while the control collapses one loop by step
${\sim}800$ and never recovers it. Metric pressure
on birth/death values suits 1-D features in a way budget allocation does
not: a loop has minimal spatial support, so allocation can only over- or
under-tessellate its neighbourhood (manufacturing phantoms or thinning the
tube), while value pressure moves, merges, or tightens the loop directly
(\S\ref{sec:discussion}). Voids, whose support is the whole shell,
respond to both channels --- why the composition (C5) wins everywhere.

\paragraph{Components (H0): not topological --- third channel, same answer.}
C1 reduces the (already small) baseline error $9.5\times$, but the generic
control matches it on the diagram metric (.0008 vs .0007); the topological
increment appears only in Chamfer ($0.54$ vs $1.94$). Three channels now
agree on the feature class:
$\beta_0$ structure is carried by the count and spatial separation of
shells --- properties \emph{any} spreading pressure improves and the
photometric term already infers from images. Topological guidance earns
its keep where geometry and topology decouple: loops and voids.

\paragraph{Composition (C5).} Loss $+$ spread prior is best everywhere
tested --- sphere $.0082$ ($7.6\times$ vs C0, ${\approx}2\times$ vs C1 alone,
${\approx}4.4\times$ below the prior alone
% src: h2_unified/report/results.json (B4 sphere .0357; .0357/.0082 = 4.37)
), torus $.0133$ --- with the best sphere Chamfer as well; on the torus its
Chamfer ($.502$) trails only the no-ramp arm ($.495$). Allocation and
value-tuning are complementary mechanisms, not substitutes.
% src: topo3/report/results.json (chamfer_mean per arm)

\paragraph{Stretch case (genus 2): saturated, honestly.} On the double torus
every arm --- baseline, loss, control --- returns a bottleneck of exactly
$.0264$ (sd $0$ over seeds), and Wasserstein is equally pinned
($.0527$--$.0528$): the two tube loops are unexpressable at feasible budgets
(replicating the allocation study's floor, suppl.\ \S A), and they dominate
both diagram metrics regardless of guidance. The loss adds no phantoms
($\#$sig $\beta_1$ $=2$ every run) at marginally best Chamfer;
the case measures the \emph{representation's} ceiling, not the
channel's --- both floors are quantified in \S\ref{sec:discussion}.
% src: topo3/report/{results.json,cache/double_torus_*} (W2 check 2026-07-07)

\paragraph{Ablations.} $\rho$-response flat over $\{0.03,0.1,0.3\}$
($.0181/.0172/.0177$ in the matched seed-0 sweep on the sphere; the
five-seed $\rho{=}0.1$ arm is Table~\ref{tab:main}'s C1); C3 (no
curriculum) $\approx$ C1 on both
decisive shapes \emph{and} off the analytic family (bob: C3
$.0171{\pm}.0026$ vs C1 $.0208{\pm}.0008$) --- the curriculum-free
finding generalizes.

Overhead, honestly: the loss adds a \emph{fixed}
${\approx}46$\,s per 2{,}500-step run (refresh $174$\,ms at $M{=}2048$
every 10 steps) --- more than the $33$--$41$\,s photometric baseline on
these deliberately tiny scenes, but the cost scales with $M$
($O(M\log M)$ per refresh), not scene or image complexity, so it
amortizes where rendering dominates; no trainable parameters are added.

\paragraph{Recruitment is load-bearing for loops (C6).} Removing the
recruitment term (pure matching $+$ diagonal, all else identical) collapses
the torus win entirely: $.0411\pm.0038$, statistically baseline
($0.6\sigma$ from C0, $12.4\sigma$ worse than C1) at baseline Chamfer ---
though the loops still \emph{exist} ($\#$sig H1 $= 2$ in every seed;
Fig.~\ref{fig:series}, right: the C6 curve rides the baseline). The run
logs locate the mechanism: one torus loop is significant at every refresh,
the other chronically \emph{below} threshold (all five seeds, all 200
refreshes) --- matching and diagonal pressure act only on the measured
loop, recruitment is the sole gradient path to the other; on this class it
\emph{is} the mechanism,
not a cold-start device. On the sphere the void is always live and matched,
recruitment never fires (0 of 200 refreshes, all seeds), so C6 is
loss-identical to C1 by construction; its tail ($.0166$ vs $.0156$,
$1.5\sigma$) doubles as a measurement of pure run-to-run (CUDA) noise.

\paragraph{Sensor-noise stress (C7/C7h).} With the plan's cloud perturbed
at every refresh ($\sigma = 0.5\%$/$1\%$ of the diagonal ${\approx}$
$0.5$/$1.0$ median sample spacings, \S\ref{sec:setup}), the loss degrades
gradually and never harms: torus $.0210{\pm}.0018$ / $.0259{\pm}.0022$
($2.0\times$/$1.6\times$ below baseline, vs C1's $2.3\times$), sphere
$.0235{\pm}.0009$ / $.0328{\pm}.0013$ ($2.7\times$/$1.9\times$, vs
$4.0\times$) --- at equal-or-better Chamfer, with the correct
significant-feature count in every run (Fig.~\ref{fig:series}). Internals:
zero Gabriel-gate failures across all $2{,}400$ noisy refreshes, and
recruitment absorbed the noise (at $\sigma{=}1\%$ the torus's second loop
is sub-threshold at every refresh; zero unreached targets).
Calibration is the noisiest part ($\lambda_{\mathrm{peak}}$ spreads
${\approx}3\times$ across seeds), yet tails stay tight --- consistent with
the flat $\rho$ response.

\subsection{Generality: external genus-known meshes}
\label{sec:generality}
Everything above is on one analytic family, so we repeat the verdict
protocol --- C0/C1/C2, three seeds, class budgets, $\rho$ and ramp
unchanged, density fixed by the floor rule (\S\ref{sec:setup}) --- on
four external genus-known meshes: \emph{spot} (0, organic), \emph{bob}
(1, organic), \emph{fandisk} (0, CAD), and the \emph{tom-yum pot}
(3, artist-authored, certified by ingest; \S\ref{sec:setup}). No
per-shape tuning of any kind.
% src: topo3/report/results.json (spot/bob/fandisk aggregates + verdicts);
% tom-yum pot = internal shape key kinkin: topo3/quicklook.json (per-seed
% tails/chamfer/nsig, 2026-07-10)
All four \textbf{pass} the verdict rule at equal-or-\emph{better} Chamfer
(Table~\ref{tab:gen}; trajectories in suppl.\ Fig.~S2), and the control's
failure modes replicate exactly: repulsion erases the void on all three H2
shapes ($\beta_2\,1{\to}0$, all seeds; on the tom-yum pot at $1.36\times$
worse Chamfer)
and collapses a loop on the genus-1 shape ($\beta_1\,2{\to}1$, all seeds).

Four observations. \emph{The H1 claim survives the family change}: bob
halves loop error with zero phantom handles in every seed ($26.1\sigma$),
replicating torus. \emph{Sharp-featured CAD benefits most}: fandisk's
$10.4\times$ exceeds every analytic-family effect. \emph{Complex organic
geometry exposes a count-level limit}: spot's \emph{baseline} manufactures a
phantom void in two of three seeds (final $\#$sig H2 $= 2,1,2$); the loss
clears it in two seeds but retains one spurious bar in the third ($1,2,1$)
--- value repair is reliable, count repair on sub-budget organic detail
(legs, horns at $N{=}1200$) is an improvement, not a guarantee; spot's C1
tail is pinned across seeds (sd $0$ at $.0237$) --- a geometric floor of
the double-torus kind, far milder.
% src: topo3/report/results.json (nsig_final; tails)
\emph{And the pipeline reaches raw artist content}: the tom-yum pot
(Fig.~\ref{fig:tomyum}) extends the wave in two directions at once ---
input class (a raw Blender export, certified rather than constructed,
\S\ref{sec:setup}) and observable (an H2 void clearing the measurement
floor only at $M{=}8192$) --- both chosen by the pre-registered rule, not
by hand: the dress rehearsal for scan data, whose ground truth must
likewise be certified. The loss cuts the void's
value error $3.9\times$ at Chamfer parity ($0.99\times$ baseline); the
$80.1\sigma$ Welch statistic reflects an unusually tight seed spread at
$n{=}3$ (C1 sd $.0002$), so the honest summary is \emph{non-overlapping
seed ranges}, with the effect size inside the study's $2.1$--$10.4\times$
span. The control \emph{destroys} the void ($\beta_2\,1{\to}0$ in all
three seeds) at $1.36\times$ worse Chamfer; its bottleneck pins at the
unmatched-target bound (the target bar's half-life, $.0223$), so the count
--- not the meaningless value gap ($0.8\sigma$) --- is the verdict. Two
caveats transfer: the baseline already reads the correct count (a
value-accuracy win, like bob), and ground truth is certified, not
constructed.
