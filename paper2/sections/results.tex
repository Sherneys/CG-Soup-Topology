\subsection{Gate: the loss alone repairs topology}
Before integration, the loss (no photometric term, Adam directly on point
positions) must repair defective clouds. It does, on all four probes
(Fig.~\ref{fig:toys}):
% src: figures/phase3_toy/t{1..4}.json (PHASE3_PLAN.md Appendix A)
a punctured sphere's void birth is pulled to target ($8.8\times$ bottleneck
reduction; the hole closes), a spurious handle is pushed to the diagonal and
dies (H1 bar eliminated; $\beta$ restored), mis-spaced components are pulled
to the target separation ($1229\times$), and --- the recruitment probe --- a
bridged merge with \emph{no} significant live H0 bar is severed
($450\times$, $\beta_0\,1{\to}2$). The predicted location-blind pathology did
not materialize, for a mechanism reason worth stating: tearing a 2-manifold
shell cannot grow a component bar past the local detour scale (the tug is
abandoned), while cutting the 1-D bridge raises the recruited bar's death
monotonically toward target --- the runaway concentrates on the only
profitable direction.

\paragraph{Voids (H2): topology-specific, large.} Sphere $4.0\times$
($16.2\sigma$ Welch vs C0), cube $7.9\times$ ($18.5\sigma$), both at
equal-or-better Chamfer (sphere $0.46$ vs $0.50$; cube $0.92$ vs $1.05$). The
norm-matched control is not merely weaker --- it is \emph{destructive}: it
pins the void's death at its repulsion equilibrium (bottleneck $2.2\times$
worse than baseline, identical across seeds) and on the cube erases the void
outright. Nor is this an artifact of control strength: a gentle variant at
half the push radius still lands $1.6\times$ \emph{worse} than baseline
(.0972 vs .0623) --- the sphere ordering is C1 $<$ C0 $<$ C2g $<$ C2, so no
tested repulsion strength reproduces any of the gain
(Fig.~\ref{fig:tails}); Appendix~\ref{app:diagnostics} visualizes the
mechanism as diagram trajectories --- the control \emph{pins and drags}
the void's death while phantom bars accumulate.

\paragraph{Loops (H1): the channel's distinguishing result.} In the
allocation-channel study (Appendix~\ref{app:companion}) no prior shape won
this class --- concentration manufactured phantom handles; spreading never
beat a random control. The loss cuts torus
H1 error $2.3\times$ with \emph{zero phantom handles in all five seeds}
(significant-count trajectories flat at the true $\beta_1{=}2$,
Fig.~\ref{fig:nsig}), while the control collapses one loop. Metric pressure
on birth/death values suits 1-D features in a way budget allocation does
not, and the reason is dimensional: a loop has minimal spatial support ---
it is characterized almost entirely by its birth/death values --- so
allocation can only over- or under-tessellate its neighbourhood
(manufacturing phantoms or thinning the tube), while value pressure moves,
merges, or tightens the loop directly. Voids, whose support is the whole
shell, respond to both channels --- which is why the composition (C5) wins
everywhere.

\paragraph{Components (H0): not topological --- third channel, same answer.}
C1 reduces the (already small) baseline error $9.5\times$, but the generic
control matches it on the diagram metric (.0008 vs .0007); the topological
increment appears only in Chamfer ($0.54$ vs $1.94$). Together with the
allocation-channel results this is now a statement about the feature class:
component structure is helped by any spreading pressure, not by topological
information. The mechanism is that $\beta_0$ structure is carried entirely
by the count and spatial separation of shells --- a property \emph{any}
spreading pressure improves by default, and one the photometric term
already infers from images. Topological guidance earns its keep where
geometry and topology decouple: loops and voids.

\paragraph{Composition (C5).} Loss $+$ spread prior is best everywhere
tested --- sphere $.0082$ ($7.6\times$ vs C0, ${\approx}2\times$ vs C1 alone,
${\approx}4.4\times$ below the prior alone
% src: h2_unified/report/results.json (B4 sphere .0357; .0357/.0082 = 4.37)
), torus $.0133$ --- with the best sphere Chamfer as well; on the torus its
Chamfer ($.502$) trails only the no-ramp arm ($.495$). Allocation and
value-tuning are complementary mechanisms, not substitutes.
% src: topo3/report/results.json (chamfer_mean per arm)

\paragraph{Stretch case (genus 2): saturated, honestly.} On the double torus
every arm --- baseline, loss, control --- returns a bottleneck of exactly
$.0264$ (sd $0$ over seeds), and Wasserstein is equally pinned
($.0527$--$.0528$): the two tube loops are unexpressable at feasible budgets
(replicating the allocation study's floor, Appendix~\ref{app:companion}),
and they dominate both diagram
metrics regardless of guidance. The loss adds no phantoms there
($\beta_1$-significant count $=2$ in every run) and its Chamfer is marginally
best; the case measures the \emph{representation's} ceiling, not the
channel's --- both floors (measurement and representation) are quantified
in \S\ref{sec:discussion}.

\paragraph{Ablations.} $\rho$-response flat over $\{0.03,0.1,0.3\}$

Overhead, honestly: the loss adds a \emph{fixed}
${\approx}46$\,s per 2{,}500-step run (refresh $174$\,ms at $M{=}2048$

\paragraph{Recruitment is load-bearing for loops (C6).} Removing the
recruitment term (pure matching $+$ diagonal, all else identical) collapses
the torus win entirely: $.0411\pm.0038$, statistically baseline
($0.6\sigma$ from C0, $12.4\sigma$ worse than C1) at baseline Chamfer ---
though the loops still \emph{exist} ($\#$sig H1 $= 2$ in every seed;
Fig.~\ref{fig:series}, right: the C6 curve rides the baseline). The run
logs locate the mechanism precisely: at the loss's own sampling density,
one torus loop is significant at every refresh while the other sits
chronically \emph{below} the significance threshold --- in all five seeds,
at all 200 refreshes --- so matching and diagonal pressure act only on the
loop the live diagram already measures, and recruitment is the sole
gradient path to the other. It is not a cold-start device; on this class it
\emph{is} the mechanism. On the sphere the void is always live and matched,
recruitment never fires (0 of 200 refreshes, all seeds), and C6 is
loss-identical to C1 by construction --- its tail ($.0166$ vs C1's $.0156$,
$1.5\sigma$) doubles as a measurement of pure run-to-run (CUDA) noise.

\paragraph{Sensor-noise stress (C7/C7h).} With the plan's cloud perturbed
at every refresh ($\sigma = 0.5\%$/$1\%$ of the diagonal ${\approx}$
$0.5$/$1.0$ median sample spacings, \S\ref{sec:setup}), the loss degrades
gradually and never harms: torus $.0210{\pm}.0018$ / $.0259{\pm}.0022$
($2.0\times$/$1.6\times$ below baseline, vs C1's $2.3\times$), sphere
$.0235{\pm}.0009$ / $.0328{\pm}.0013$ ($2.7\times$/$1.9\times$, vs
$4.0\times$) --- at equal-or-better Chamfer, with the correct
significant-feature count in every run (Fig.~\ref{fig:series}). The
internals explain the grace: across all $2{,}400$ noisy refreshes
\emph{not one} significant pair failed the Gabriel gate --- the detachment
fallback of \S\ref{sec:method} was never needed --- and recruitment
absorbed the noise: at $\sigma{=}1\%$ the torus's second loop is
sub-threshold at every refresh and recruitment reaches it every time
(zero unreached targets throughout). Calibration is the noisiest part
($\lambda_{\mathrm{peak}}$ spreads ${\approx}3\times$ across seeds), yet
tails stay tight --- consistent with the flat $\rho$ response.

\subsection{Generality: external genus-known meshes}
\label{sec:generality}
Everything above is on one analytic scene family. We therefore repeat the
verdict protocol --- C0/C1/C2, three seeds, class budgets, $\rho$ and ramp
unchanged, sampling density fixed by the pre-registered floor rule (the
$2048$ default everywhere except the tom-yum pot's $8192$,
\S\ref{sec:setup}) --- on
four external meshes of known genus (\S\ref{sec:setup}): \emph{spot}
(genus 0, organic), \emph{bob} (genus 1, organic), the \emph{fandisk} CAD
benchmark (genus 0), and the \emph{tom-yum pot} (genus 3, artist-authored,
certified by ingest). No per-shape tuning of any kind: the headroom budgets
transfer from the feature class, bob runs the full unrestricted bundle, and
the pot's density is dictated by the rule, not chosen.
% src: topo3/report/results.json (spot/bob/fandisk aggregates + verdicts);
% tom-yum pot = internal shape key kinkin: topo3/quicklook.json (per-seed
% tails/chamfer/nsig, 2026-07-10)
All four \textbf{pass} the verdict rule at equal-or-\emph{better} Chamfer
(Table~\ref{tab:gen}, Fig.~\ref{fig:gen}), and the control's failure modes
replicate exactly: repulsion erases the void on all three H2 shapes
($\beta_2\,1{\to}0$, all seeds; on the tom-yum pot at $1.36\times$ worse
Chamfer)
and collapses a loop on the genus-1 shape ($\beta_1\,2{\to}1$, all seeds).

Four observations. \emph{The H1 claim survives the family change}: bob
halves loop error with zero phantom handles in every seed ($26.1\sigma$),
replicating torus. \emph{Sharp-featured CAD benefits most}: fandisk's
$10.4\times$ exceeds every analytic-family effect. \emph{Complex organic
geometry exposes a count-level limit}: spot's \emph{baseline} manufactures a
phantom void in two of three seeds (final $\#$sig H2 $= 2,1,2$); the loss
clears it in two seeds but retains one spurious bar in the third ($1,2,1$)
--- value repair is reliable, count repair on sub-budget organic detail
(legs, horns at $N{=}1200$) is an improvement, not a guarantee. Spot's C1
tail is also pinned across seeds (sd $0$ at $.0237$), suggesting a geometric
floor of the same kind as the double torus, far milder in degree.
\emph{And the pipeline reaches raw artist content}: the tom-yum pot passes
the same
rule with its observable and sampling density chosen by the pre-registered
floor rule rather than by hand --- the dress rehearsal for scan data, whose
ground truth must likewise be certified, not assumed (\S\ref{sec:setup}).

\paragraph{From artist soup to verdict (the tom-yum pot).} The certified
artist mesh
(Fig.~\ref{fig:tomyum}) extends the wave in two directions at once: the
input class (a raw Blender export, certified rather than constructed,
\S\ref{sec:setup}) and the observable (an H2 void that only clears the
measurement floor at $M{=}8192$). The loss cuts the void's value error
$3.9\times$ at Chamfer parity ($0.99\times$ baseline); the $80.1\sigma$
Welch statistic reflects an unusually tight seed spread at $n{=}3$ (C1 sd
$.0002$), so the honest summary is \emph{non-overlapping seed ranges}, with
the effect size inside the study's $2.1$--$10.4\times$ span. The control
\emph{destroys} the void ($\beta_2\,1{\to}0$ in all three seeds) at
$1.36\times$ worse Chamfer; its bottleneck pins at the unmatched-target
bound (the target bar's half-life, $.0223$), so the count --- not the
meaningless value gap ($0.8\sigma$) --- is the verdict. Two caveats
transfer verbatim from the rest of the study: the baseline already reads
the correct count (a value-accuracy win, like bob), and ground truth here
is certified, not constructed.
