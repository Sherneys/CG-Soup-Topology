We moved topology out of the resampler's allocation channel into the
training objective. Four things carry it: a matched
persistence loss whose
pair-frozen backward is \emph{certified} per pairing by a Gabriel-exactness
gate; a recruitment term restoring the gradient optimal matching
provably lacks for missing features (load-bearing for
loops); channel-controlled evaluation (every gain beats a norm-matched
non-topological control at Chamfer parity); and a quantified account of
the floors (measurement, $6\,r_{\mathrm{med}}(M)$; representation,
budget $N$). The verdicts replicate, without per-shape
tuning, on external genus-known meshes (one a raw artist mesh certified
by measurement) and degrade gracefully under noise.
The prescription: correct topology in the \emph{loss}, allocate with a
\emph{wide} prior, combine; next: open surfaces and real scans.
