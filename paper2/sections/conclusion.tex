We moved topology out of the resampler's allocation channel into the
training objective. Four things carry it: a matched
persistence loss whose
pair-frozen backward is \emph{certified} per pairing by a Gabriel-exactness
gate; a recruitment term restoring the gradient optimal matching
provably lacks for missing features --- the load-bearing mechanism for
loops; a channel-controlled evaluation where every gain must beat
a norm-matched non-topological control at Chamfer parity; and a quantified
account of the floors (measurement, $6\,r_{\mathrm{med}}(M)$;
representation, budget $N$). The verdicts replicate
on external genus-known meshes --- including a raw artist mesh certified
by measurement --- without per-shape tuning, and degrade gracefully
under noise.
The prescription: correct topology in the \emph{loss}, allocate with a
\emph{wide} prior, combine. Future work: open surfaces and real scans,
adaptive sampling density, template-free targets, curvature-weighted
masking.
