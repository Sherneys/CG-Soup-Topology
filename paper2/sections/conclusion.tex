\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

% Added per advisor round 2 (the paper previously ended at Limitations).
% TODO(human): voice pass.

This paper moved topology out of the resampler's allocation channel and
into the training objective of a differentiable triangle-soup pipeline.
Four things carry the contribution: a matched persistence loss whose
pair-frozen backward is \emph{certified} per pairing by a Gabriel-exactness
gate; a recruitment term that restores the gradient optimal matching
provably lacks for missing features --- and which an ablation shows is the
load-bearing mechanism for the loop class; a channel-controlled evaluation
in which every gain must beat a norm-matched non-topological control at
Chamfer parity; and a quantified account of the method's floors (the
$6\,r_{\mathrm{med}}(M)$ measurement floor and the budget-$N$
representation ceiling). The verdicts replicate on external genus-known
meshes without per-shape tuning and degrade gracefully under sensor noise.
The prescription stands: correct topology in the \emph{loss}, allocate
budget with a \emph{wide} prior, and combine them --- the two channels are
complementary, and the composition is best everywhere tested. Future work:
open surfaces and real scans (boundary-aware persistence), adaptive or
multi-scale sampling density, template-free targets (genus-level
penalties), and curvature-weighted spatial masking of the loss.
